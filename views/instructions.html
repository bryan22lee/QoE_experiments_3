Instructions

This survey studies how video quality damages occuring at different times affect your video-watching experience differently. The list of videos you will be asked to watch and grade are created from a same original video by inserting quality damages at different times. This survey is divided to two sections:

  • First part is a short training, where you will learn how to recognize video damage and how to grade them.
    1. You will first watch two short example videos. These examples show you two types of quality damage you might see in this study: resolution decrease, and video pause.
    2. We use a 5-level grading: Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor, or Bad. After watching the short videos, you will then watch another two reference videos. The first is an example of "Excellent" grade (no perceptible damage). The second is an example of "Bad" grade (annoying damage).
  • Second part is where you will grade the test videos.
    1. You should grade the quality of each test video by comparing to the two reference videos you have watched.

Please be sure to watch each video in its full length and answer every question in this survey honestly.

Please take the simple survey before the test.
